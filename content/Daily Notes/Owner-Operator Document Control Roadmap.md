# **Owner / Operator Document Control Roadmap**

---

## **Purpose**                                                                  

This document establishes a **phased, realistic roadmap** for defining, gathering, controlling, and maintaining owner/operator documentation for a specialty chemical manufacturing environment.

It is explicitly designed for:

- An **owner/operator with in-house EPC capability**
- **Limited historical standards and legacy data**
- **PSM-covered and non-PSM processes**
- **Severely constrained personnel resources**

The roadmap begins with a **Minimal Viable Evergreen** document set and intentionally grows into a **Lifecycle Authoritative Document Control & Information Management (DCIM)** function.

This is not a theoretical EPC ideal. It is a survival‑grade path that reduces operational, safety, and capital risk while remaining achievable.

---

## **1. First Principles (Non Negotiable Framing)**

Before defining deliverables, the organization must align on the following realities:

- This is an **owner/operator record set**, not a project deliverables list
- **Visibility and trustworthiness** matter more than theoretical completeness
- **PSM-covered processes define the minimum bar**, not the exception
- If Operations and Maintenance **cannot find or trust a document, it does not exist**
- Engineering drawings without revision authority are **reference material, not records**

Fast growth specialty chemical facilities commonly reach a point where:

- Knowledge is tribal
- Documents exist but are not authoritative
- Changes are made faster than records are updated

This roadmap assumes that condition explicitly and works forward from it.

---

## **2. Regulatory, PSM, and RAGAGEP Anchors**

The following drivers establish _why_ owner maintained documentation is required, regardless of staffing or maturity:

- OSHA Process Safety Management (29 CFR 1910.119)
- EPA Risk Management Plans (40 CFR Part 68)
- RAGAGEP expectations derived from recognized industry standards

Key implications:

- Process Safety Information (PSI) must be **accurate, accessible, and current**
- Hazard analyses must reference **current process definitions**
- Operating and maintenance decisions must be traceable to authoritative records

The organization does **not** need to adopt every industry standard. It **must** demonstrate that it understands which standards apply, where it aligns, and where it has consciously deviated.

## **3. Core Owner / Operator Document Categories**

**Legend for Scope & Origin**

**[E] Engineering Handover Deliverable** – Produced by Engineering / EPC and formally handed over as a record

**[O] Operations-Owned Record** – Owned, maintained, and governed by Operations

**[E→O] Engineered Input, Operationally Declared** – Derived from engineering and safety work, formally owned by Operations

**[EHS] EHS / Process Safety–Owned Record** – Governed by EHS / Process Safety for regulatory continuity and risk management

These categories reflect **how Operations and Maintenance interact with the plant**, not how EPC disciplines organize work.

**Policy – Project Artifacts vs Evergreen Records**

_Interface, tie-in, demolition, and temporary state records are authoritative only for the duration of change and must be reconciled into evergreen plant records prior to project closeout._

### **4. Document Control Reality for a Lean Organization**

This roadmap assumes:

- No existing formal document control group
- Limited personnel capacity
- No appetite for heavyweight bureaucracy

Minimum viable requirements:

- A **single authoritative repository**
- Clear **revision rules**
- Defined **document owners** by class
- Explicit distinction between **master records** and **project copies**

Start with documents that:

- Are safety critical
- Are repeatedly used
- Feed future capital work

Perfection is not required. Authority is.

**Controlled Imperfection vs Uncontrolled Accuracy**

Documents entering control under this roadmap are **not required to be fully reconciled or as‑built at the moment authority is asserted**.

The organization explicitly recognizes three states of documentation:

1. **Uncontrolled** – Multiple copies exist, currency is unknown, and changes propagate informally.
2. **Controlled but known imperfect** – A single authoritative version exists, known gaps or limitations are visible, and all future changes are governed.
3. **Reconciled / as-built** – Document accuracy has been verified against field conditions.

Phase 1 intentionally moves documents from **uncontrolled** to **controlled but known‑imperfect**. This arrests further degradation and creates a stable baseline from which reconciliation can occur.

Reconciliation is performed **selectively and deliberately**, triggered by:

- Management of Change affecting the area
- Safety or reliability decisions that depend on accuracy
- Project closeout and turnover gates

Requiring full reconciliation as a prerequisite to control is explicitly rejected. Uncontrolled accuracy presents greater operational and safety risk than controlled imperfection with visible limitations.

# **Phased Roadmap: From Minimal Viable Evergreen to Lifecycle Authority**

---

## **Phase 0 – Declare Reality (Alignment Phase)**

**Objective**: Create organizational permission to treat documentation as a risk issue, not clerical overhead.

Key actions:

- Acknowledge that no authoritative owner record set exists today
- Agree on definitions:

- Authoritative vs reference
- Evergreen vs as‑built

- Identify an executive sponsor for document authority

**Exit condition**: Leadership alignment that document accuracy is a safety, operability, and capital risk issue.

---

## **Phase 1 – Minimal Viable Evergreen**

**Objective**: Establish a small, defensible core of documents that make the physical plant and its control behavior immediately visible and trustworthy to Operations and Maintenance.

**Scope (example)**:

- P&IDs (master) **[E]**
- Plot plans and general arrangements **[E]**
- Control narratives **[E]**
- Cause & effect diagrams **[E]**
- Electrical single lines **[E]**
- Area classification drawings **[E]**
- Relief system basis **[E]**
- PSI index **[EHS]**

**Governance rules**:

- One authoritative repository
- One revision scheme
- One owner per document class
- Changes flow through MOC or they do not exist

**Explicit exclusions**:

- No formal turnover packages
- No contractor submittal governance
- No lifecycle metadata optimization

**Exit condition**: Operations and Maintenance can reliably find and trust the core document set.

---

## **Phase 2 – Controlled Expansion (Bridge Phase)**

**Objective**: Extend authority only where lifecycle risk demands it.

**Expanded scope**:

- Equipment list **[E]**
- Line list **[E]**
- Instrument index **[E]**
- Electrical load lists **[E]**
- Brownfield interface registers **[E→O]**
- Tie-in registers **[E→O]**
- Demolition and temporary state records (as records) **[E→O]**

**Key shifts**:

- Registers become authoritative
- Clear separation between design churn and record control

**Exit condition**: Projects can hand over clean, bounded record updates without heroic effort.

---

## **Phase 3 – Formal Handover & Turnover**

**Objective**: Make handover boring and repeatable.

Introduced controls:

- Standard turnover package definition
- Reconciliation checklists
- As-built acceptance gates
- Document Control sign‑off as closeout prerequisite

**Exit condition**: Every project leaves the plant better documented than it found it.

---

## **Phase 4 – Lifecycle‑Authoritative DCIM**

**Objective**: Treat information as infrastructure.

Characteristics:

- Document Control owns lifecycle authority
- Engineering owns technical content
- Operations consumes records without translation
- Audits verify rather than investigate

**Steady state outcome**: Documentation compounds in value instead of decaying.

---

# **Final Note**

The most common failure mode is freezing at Phase 1 and calling it pragmatic.

Phase 1 only succeeds if:

- It is explicitly incomplete
- It delivers visible operational value
- It has a published path forward

This roadmap exists to prevent that stall while respecting real staffing and cultural constraints.

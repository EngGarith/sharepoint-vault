# **Executive Summary**

## **Owner / Operator Document Control Roadmap**

---

**Why This Matters**

The company has grown rapidly over the past 15 years without a formal owner‑operator document control function. As a result:

- Critical plant information exists but is not consistently **authoritative**
- Engineering, Operations, Maintenance, and EHS rely on **different versions of the truth**
- Changes are made faster than records are updated, increasing safety, operability, and capital risk

This roadmap establishes a **realistic, phased approach** to regain control of plant documentation without requiring large upfront staffing or disruptive rework.

---

**What This Roadmap Is (and Is Not)**

**This roadmap is:**

- An owner/operator framework, not an EPC deliverables list
- A method to **stop document degradation first**, then improve accuracy over time
- Explicitly aligned with PSM, RAGAGEP, and regulatory expectations
- Designed for limited resources and brownfield reality

**This roadmap is not:**

- A demand for immediate field reconciliation or as‑built perfection
- A document cleanup or scanning initiative
- A software or tooling selection effort

---

**Core Principle**

**Control comes before reconciliation.**

Documents do not need to be perfect before they are governed. The organization explicitly recognizes three documentation states:

1. Uncontrolled
2. Controlled but known‑imperfect
3. Reconciled / as‑built

Phase 1 moves critical documents from uncontrolled to controlled, arresting further degradation. Reconciliation occurs deliberately when work touches the plant or projects close.

---

## **What Is Being Defined**

The roadmap separates two concerns:

- **What the owner/operator ultimately must govern** (complete document universe)
- **When the organization is ready to assert authority over each class of document** (phased activation)

Not all documents are activated at once by design.

---

### **Document Ownership Model (Simplified)**

- **Engineering [E]** – Produces and hands over authoritative technical records
- **Operations [O]** – Owns how the plant is run day‑to‑day
- **EHS / Process Safety [EHS]** – Governs regulatory continuity and risk memory
- **Engineered → Operational [E→O]** – Engineered constraints declared and owned operationally

Authority is based on lifecycle responsibility, not who originally created the document.

---

### **Phased Implementation (High Level)**

#### **Phase 0 – Alignment**

- Acknowledge current reality
- Establish common definitions
- Assign executive sponsorship

#### **Phase 1 – Minimal Viable Evergreen**

- Make the plant and its control behavior visible and trustworthy
- Control high‑value drawings and safety boundaries
- Stop further document degradation

#### **Phase 2 – Controlled Expansion**

- Introduce authoritative registers and interface control
- Manage brownfield risk during change

#### **Phase 3 – Formal Handover**

- Standardize turnover
- Force reconciliation at project closeout

#### **Phase 4 – Lifecycle Authority**

- Treat information as infrastructure
- Documentation compounds instead of decays

---

**What Success Looks Like**

- Operations and Maintenance can **find and trust** plant information
- Projects leave the plant **better documented than they found it**
- Safety and compliance records persist independent of personnel changes
- Engineering effort shifts from rediscovery to value‑added design

---

**What We Need from Leadership**

For this roadmap to succeed, a small number of **explicit leadership commitments** are required. These are not budget-heavy, but they are authority-critical.

Leadership is asked to:

- **Name and visibly support an executive sponsor** for owner documentation authority
- **Reinforce that controlled-but-imperfect documents are acceptable**, and preferred over uncontrolled accuracy
- **Back document authority decisions** when they create short-term inconvenience or slow work
- **Hold projects accountable for record reconciliation at closeout**, not after the fact
- **Resist expanding Phase 1 scope** beyond what staffing and governance can honestly support

Without leadership reinforcement, this initiative will fail quietly. With it, the organization can regain control of plant knowledge **incrementally and sustainably**.

---

**Bottom Line**

This roadmap does not lower standards. It **sequences reality**.

By asserting control first and improving accuracy deliberately, the organization reduces operational risk immediately while building toward a sustainable, authoritative owner record system.
